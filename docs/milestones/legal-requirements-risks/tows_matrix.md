# TOWS Matrix
Moving beyond simply listing factors, TOWS matrix combines our internal traits with external market conditions to generate business and development strategies

## SO Strategy (Strengths + Opportunities)

- **Action**: Leverage our **Native AAOS Integration (S)** and the **AAOS Market Growth (O)** to position the app as a specialized "Smart City" out-of-the-box solution for OEMs. Instead of competing with mobile apps, we market the software as a native V2X safety layer that integrates directly with IoT infrastructures.

## ST Strategy (Strengths + Threats)

- **Action**: Use our **Hyper-Local Safety Intelligence (S)** to combat **Big Tech Competition (T)**. Giant navigation apps (Waze/TomTom) rely on delayed crowdsourced data. We can explicitly market the app's unique capability to provide instantaneous, sensor-verified warnings (e.g., the overtaking assistant) that fundamentally outpaces crowdsourced data in life-saving reactivity.

## WO Strategy (Weaknesses + Opportunities)

- **Action**: Overcome our **Zero Brand Recognition (W)** by capitalizing on **Smart City Initiatives (O)**. We can partner directly with the **Aveiro Municipality** or **Portugal 2030** mobility programs. By becoming an **"Official City Partner"** for traffic safety, we gain instant credibility and user adoption without needing a huge marketing budget.

## WT Strategy (Weaknesses + Threats)

- **Action**: To protect against our **Infrastructure Dependence (W)** and **API Pricing Volatility (T)**, we will need a robust offline caching and fallback mechanisms. If **ITAv** sensors drop or MapTiler blocks our API key, the app must gracefully degrade into a standard, cached offline map rather than crashing, ensuring the driver is never left "blind".
